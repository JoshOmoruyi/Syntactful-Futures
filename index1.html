A little background about me...

I am currently a senior computer science student at my University. While my efforts and practices have brought me to a good standing, I can say that the ride was not a smooth one.

Before college, I was only exposed to computer science one time, yes, one time. I was lucky to be able to register for a Mobile CSP Course at my high school my freshman year. This course was my true introduction to computer science and inspired me to continue to pursue it as a major. Unfortunately, this course was later removed as the only available professor had other obligations that conflicted with our school, and a replacement was never sought out. Just like that, a candle was blown out.

My first semester as a Computer Science student was rough. I felt a constant overwhelmingness and my confidence lacked, while my peers who came from different areas were able to work through problems diligently.

It took me a while to understand and grasp concepts, but after getting the opportunity to meet some of my classmates, I came to a realization that my high school could have better prepared us for STEM majors such as Computer Science.

Fast forward to 2025, a quick browse of my high school's website informs me that there is now a computer science department listed under STEM.

I am glad that local schooling is finally taking the steps to prepare young students for the next level so they can feel confident, and that is what I intend to do with this program.

Where schools lack, this program aims to build confidence in the coding skills of kids and teens through the use of hands-on practices.

AI

Artificial Intelligence is a pivotal resource functioning in the median between humans and technology. An AI is a machine that is programmed to handle tasks ranging from problem solving, image generation, automation, and more.

As we move further to the future, the capability of AI continues to reach new heights. As programmers, it is important not to feel threatened by AI, but to instead work with AI to increase our understanding.

Big Data

Big Data refers to extremely large datasets that are too complex to process with traditional data processing tools. Big data technologies allow organizations to store, analyze, and visualize massive amounts of information efficiently.

Common tools in Big Data include Hadoop, Spark, and NoSQL databases, which are used for analytics, machine learning, and decision-making processes.

Cyber Security

Cyber Security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, or damage. It involves securing software, hardware, and sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Key areas include network security, encryption, threat detection, and ethical hacking to safeguard organizations and individuals from cyber threats.
